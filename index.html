Selected projects

A selection of academic and exploratory projects spanning bachelor and master level work, from data-driven analysis and automation to speculative and embodied interaction design.

Knowable – Trustpilot Review Analysis Platform

Bachelor project · Data analysis, NLP & web scraping

My role: Concept, data pipeline, text analysis and prototype.

Knowable challenges the idea that a 1–5 star Trustpilot score fully reflects customer experience. I built a web scraping and text analysis pipeline in R that collects reviews, runs sentiment and topic modelling, and presents a richer overview of what customers actually say about a company.

R Web scraping NLP Sentiment analysis Topic modelling Shiny

EchoGuide – Accessible Museum Companion

Master project · UX, accessibility & iterative prototyping

My role: Concept development, interaction design and prototype testing.

EchoGuide explores how museums can be more inclusive for visitors with visual impairments and other needs. Through several design iterations, we developed a mobile concept that uses audio guidance and recognition to read out exhibition information and support independent navigation, with scalability to other audiences such as children and dyslexic visitors.

Design sprint UX Accessibility Prototyping p5.js

LumiBreath – Interactive Breathing Lamp

Digital Designer course · Tangible interaction & wellbeing

My role: Co-design of concept, interaction and documentation.

LumiBreath is a physical-digital lamp that guides users through calming breathing exercises using light rhythms and motion. The project investigates how embodied interaction and everyday artefacts can support stress reduction and emotional regulation, drawing on third-wave HCI and theories of perception and embodiment.

Interaction design Embodied interaction Physical prototyping Arduino / hardware

Det man ikke ser – Critical Design on the Sunflower Lanyard

Master semester project · Critical & speculative design

My role: Research, experiment design and critical reflection.

This project investigates how the sunflower lanyard for hidden disabilities is interpreted in public space, and how it can both support and stigmatise its wearers. Through critical and speculative design experiments, we used prototypes as “social mirrors” to expose assumptions, the politics of staring, and the embodied experience of being visibly marked as different.

Critical design Speculative design Research-through-design Stigma & disability studies

Brøndby IF – Data-Driven Football Analytics

Bachelor project · Sports analytics & statistics

My role: Data preparation, modelling and interpretation.

In this project, we developed an Expected Goals (xG) model and a set of analytical tools to support decision-making in Brøndby IF. Using detailed event data, we analysed shot quality, patterns of play and player behaviour to show how data can complement football intuition with more systematic insight.

R Predictive modelling Clustering Sports analytics Visualisation

RPA in Barnabókaklubbin – Automation in a Children’s Book Club

Master project · Digital transformation & RPA

My role: Interviews, process mapping, design of automation approach and evaluation.

Barnabókaklubbin is a Faroese children’s book club with fragmented systems and highly manual administration. This project investigates how Robotic Process Automation can reduce repetitive work while respecting local practices and human judgement. We mapped membership workflows, assessed data quality and tested a selective automation design using RPA as a supportive layer rather than a full replacement.

Service blueprinting RPA Power Automate Qualitative research Digital transformation
