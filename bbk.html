RPA in Barnabókaklubbin – Automation in a Children’s Book Club

Master project · Digital transformation · RPA · Data & process mapping

Barnabókaklubbin is a Faroese children’s book club whose daily operations depend heavily on manual coordination across several loosely connected systems. This project investigates how Robotic Process Automation (RPA) can support their membership administration without undermining the human judgement that keeps everything running.

Service blueprinting Process mapping RPA Power Automate Qualitative research Digital transformation

Context & problem

The club manages memberships, payments and book shipments using a mix of web forms, spreadsheets, Navision/Business Central and email. Because the systems do not fully talk to each other, staff rely on:

  • Manual data entry across multiple platforms.
  • Personal knowledge of “how things are usually done”.
  • Continuous checking to catch errors and exceptions.

This creates both workload and vulnerability: if the person with the overview is away, operations become fragile.

My role

  • Conducted and analysed interviews with key staff members.
  • Co-developed service blueprints of current and future membership flows.
  • Helped identify which process steps were suitable for RPA.
  • Contributed to designing and testing a small RPA proof-of-concept.
  • Worked on evaluation and reflection on organisational implications.

Process & approach

Understanding current practice

We started by mapping how new memberships are created, updated and cancelled in practice – not just in formal descriptions. Interviews and blueprinting revealed extensive manual work and “glue tasks” performed by staff to keep data consistent.

Assessing automation potential

We analysed which steps were:

  • Rule-based and repetitive (good RPA candidates).
  • Exception-heavy and highly dependent on judgement (less suitable).

Membership registration emerged as a promising area, provided that data quality and exception handling were carefully considered.

RPA proof-of-concept

Using Power Automate, we prototyped an automation that:

  • Reads membership form submissions.
  • Transfers data into relevant systems.
  • Reduces duplicate typing and basic copy-paste work.

Outcome & insights

The project proposes a selective, modular automation strategy:

  • RPA as a supporting layer, not a full replacement for staff.
  • Focus on stabilising routine flows before tackling complex edge cases.
  • Continuous involvement of staff to surface tacit knowledge and exceptions.

We show that in small, low-maturity organisations, “big bang” automation is less realistic than incremental, context-sensitive changes.

Reflections & learnings

This project strengthened my ability to connect technical automation options (RPA) with organisational realities and data quality concerns. It also gave me experience designing automation in a way that respects human expertise instead of trying to replace it.
